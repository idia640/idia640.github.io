Paradigm shift?

Sat, Sep 10, 2016

In our second week, we talked a bit about the nature of scientists and science. In this vein, how does one recognize a Paradigm shift? Perhaps, hindsight is the only test, but in a graceful redirection, Luca Turin says, “You see, when you’re a scientist, you’re always selling ideas. And people are very resistant to ideas, and rightly so. Why should new ideas be accepted?“ Take a look at this talk about how smell works, and the power of industry in shaping scientific discourse.

What is this class about?

Wed, Aug 31, 2016

The graphic below, depicts the conceptual scaffold for this course. During the semester, we will link core cognitive and neurobiological processes that give you a palette of practical levers for use in information and interaction design. Decision Model Accordingly, you will learn how to influence user decision-making processes in addition to learning how such processes may be adapted to fit cognitive needs of specific user populations. I hope that you will also recognize the use of such levers when you are the user, as well.
